1. ProofMap

Graph of pleadings → evidence → contradictions → gaps.

Each pleaded allegation is a node. Each document/witness/expert is another node. The links say SUPPORTS, UNDERMINES, CONTRADICTS, or GAP.

1. Load-Bearing Evidence Detector

Identifies the most important evidence in the case.

Example:

“Email dated 14 March is the only contemporaneous evidence supporting the alleged deadline.”

1. Case Collapse Simulator

Lets the lawyer remove or weaken evidence to see what happens.

Examples:

Remove Witness A

Exclude Expert Report

Downgrade Email Chain as ambiguous

Assume Contract Clause 4.2 is preferred

Then Ratio shows which pleaded propositions collapse.

1. Contradiction Heatmap

Timeline with red conflict nodes.

Example:

Witness says no contact after 5 March.

Email shows contact on 7 March.

Red timeline node opens split-screen contradiction.

1. Evidence Gap Orders

Instead of just saying “unsupported”, it suggests what type of evidence is needed:

Need witness evidence

Need expert evidence

Need contemporaneous document

Need financial records

Need contractual clause

Need causation evidence

This is very lawyer-useful.
